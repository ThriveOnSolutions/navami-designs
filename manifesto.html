The manifesto

We build homes, not invoices.

Six principles. No fine print. If we ever break one, you'll find it on this page first — struck through.

01

Money not spent is yours.

We work on a Guaranteed Maximum Price. When framing comes in cheaper, when a sub forgets to bill for a Tuesday, when the AI ledger catches a duplicate order — that money lands in your account, not ours. The standard industry move is to pocket it as "contingency reserve." We think that's a quiet way to overcharge.
02

The ledger is yours, too.

Day one, you get a login. Every quote, invoice, change order, and rebate lives there in real time. Sort by trade. Export to your CPA. Ask AI a question like "why did electrical jump 9% this month?" and get a real answer in a real sentence. If a builder won't show you the books, ask why.
03

Cheap is not the goal. Right-priced is.

We don't shop for the lowest bid — we shop for the bid where the math works out for ten years, not ten days. A $40K HVAC that lasts two decades beats a $22K unit that quits on year seven. Our AI runs the lifetime math; you make the call.
04

Subcontractors get named, paid, and credited.

Every GC and trade we partner with is listed on our Partners page, paid net-15 or better, and gets a written reference at project close. A builder who burns subs builds bad homes. We've seen it. We don't do it.
05

AI handles paperwork. Humans handle taste.

Permits, COI tracking, inspection scheduling, lender draws, insurance riders — AI runs them on a quiet loop in the background. Humans pick the tile, walk the framing, and tell you when the architect's brilliant skylight is going to cook the living room every July. The boring stuff is automated. The important stuff isn't.
06

A house is plywood. A home is intention.

We build for the kitchen breakfast that happens five thousand times, not the magazine spread that happens once. Storage where you actually need it. Sightlines that work in real light. Acoustics that don't punish a phone call. If a feature doesn't earn its keep over a decade of Tuesdays, it doesn't make the plan.
We started Navami because we got tired of watching neighbors pay a quarter-million dollars for the privilege of not knowing where their money went. — Founders, Navami Designs
Why we started

One home. One conviction.

In 2023, we bought a tear-down on Grant Ave in Alamo Heights. We built a 4-bedroom, 3,300-sqft contemporary on a 0.17-acre lot. Stucco facade, standing-seam metal roof, gourmet kitchen, primary suite with treetop views from the upstairs game room.

We sold it for $1.65 million. The buyer cried at closing — happy tears, not surprise-invoice tears. That's the bar.

Then we looked around and saw most builders in South Texas still operate on faxes, gut feelings, and Excel files that nobody but the foreman can read. So we're building something different on Zambrano in Terrell Hills — and on every project after — where the ledger is software, the spend is open, and the savings come home with you.
